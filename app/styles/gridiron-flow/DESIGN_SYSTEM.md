# Gridiron Flow - Design System

## Visual Ethos
Tactical, grounded, and high-impact. Gridiron Flow is inspired by the strategic depth and physical power of American Football. The vibe is "The War Room" — a mix of high-tech analysis and the raw texture of the field. Elements feel heavy and purposeful, moving with the momentum of a fullback.

## Design Token System
- **Background**: `#121A12` (Deep Turf Green) - Base environment color.
- **Surface**: `#1E291E` (Endzone Grey) - Used for cards and elevated components.
- **Accent 1**: `#F2F2F2` (Yard-line White) - Used for primary CTAs and critical data.
- **Accent 2**: `#8B4513` (Pigskin Brown) - Used for secondary elements or highlights.
- **Border**: `rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.2)` - Subtle dividers resembling chalk lines.
- **Text High**: `#FFFFFF` - Primary headers and important text.
- **Text Low**: `#A9B2A9` - Body text and secondary information.

## Typography System
- **Primary Font**: **Big Shoulders Display** (Numbers/Headings - Condensed, Heavy)
- **Secondary Font**: **Public Sans** (Body/UI - Industrial, Clean)
- **Scale**: Hero (100px), H1 (72px), H2 (36px), Body (18px)

## Motion Language: Kinetic Design Protocol
1. **Impact Physics**: Camera shake/jolt on element entry. Elements should feel like they have mass and hit the layout with force.
2. **Yard-line Progress**: Implement a scroll-based progress indicator that stretches based on scroll velocity, resembling field yard markers.
3. **Chalk-talk Aesthetics**: Interactions and diagrams should reveal themselves as if being drawn on a chalkboard.
4. **Heavy Momentum**: Transitions should use physics-based springs with low damping and high stiffness to convey weight and power.
